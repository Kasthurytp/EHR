About the AES

AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) is a symmetric encryption algorithm widely used for securing sensitive data. It is a block cipher that operates on fixed-size blocks of data and uses a secret key for both encryption and decryption processes. AES has become the de facto standard for encryption, adopted by organizations and governments worldwide.
