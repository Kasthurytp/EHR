"Why Srilanka doest implement good EHR system

EHR stands for Electronic Health Record. It is a digital version of a patient's medical history that contains information such as their medical conditions, diagnoses, medications, allergies, immunization records, lab results, and other relevant clinical information. EHRs are used by healthcare providers to manage and coordinate a patient's care.

These records (EHRs) are increasingly being used to store and manage patient information, making healthcare more efficient and accessible. However, like any system that deals with sensitive personal information, EHRs must be secured to protect the patients' privacy and confidentiality.However, the use of EHRs also raises security concerns, as sensitive medical information can be vulnerable to unauthorized access or disclosure.With the widespread use of EHR, building a secure EHR sharing environment has attracted a lot of attention in both the healthcare industry and academic community in Srilanka.One of the biggest security issues in EHRs is data breaches in sensitive medical information.


Mission

To implement a secure and integrated Electronic Health Record (EHR) system in Sri Lanka, empowering patients and ensuring the confidentiality and security of their medical records. .

vission

To establish a standardized and widely accepted EHR sharing environment, connecting healthcare organizations for seamless communication and improved decision-making based on comprehensive patient information

End goals

To enhance healthcare delivery and patient outcomes through robust data protection, centralized storage, and efficient management of EHRs, while promoting patient privacy and instilling trust in the healthcare system.

Background idea of this project

This idea came up from one of our team mates who is experienced with this problem in public medical sectors in the northern province.because when emergency situation happens health sectors gave their treatment without aware about patient medical history and we still find that patients have to physically carry all the records of test reports and the history of health while visiting the doctors.but it is not suitable for emergency situation.Therefore We conducted surveys and interviews with the general public and healthcare providers to gain a deeper understanding of why Sri Lanka has not been able to find effective technological solutions in the medical sector, despite its advancements in other fields. This was done to address our confusion and seek clarification on the factors contributing to the lack of progress in the implementation of advanced technology in healthcare in Sri Lanka.Then we came up with the solution by our research project in the deeper need of a secure EHR system in Sri Lanka .

Research Overveiw

The implementation of Electronic Health Record systems in Sri Lanka has been attempted in various parts of the country, but it has yet to be implemented on a nationwide scale. Based on the analysis and literature review, it is widely recognized that EHR systems are at a high risk of data breaches due to the sensitive nature of the information they contain and the increasing sophistication of cybercriminals. According to a study by the Ponemon Institute, the healthcare industry experiences the highest number of data breaches of any industry, with over 60% of breaches being caused by hacking or IT incidents. .

findings

Patient Demographic Information: The system should be able to store and manage patient demographic information, such as name, address, date of birth, and contact information. Secure Access: The system should have robust security features to ensure that patient health information is protected, with data encryption, ClinicalData Management: The system should be able to store and manage patient clinical data, including medical history, current medications, laboratory test results, imaging studies, and other relevant information. User-friendly interface: The system should have a user-friendly interface that is intuitive and easy to use for clinicians, patients, and other users. Web portal accessibilty:The system should be accessible through laptop and other devices. .
